BreadHaus

Project

BreadHaus is an artisan bread brand born in New York City, blending modern design with the soul of the city.

Scope

Bag design, logo, branding, poster promotion, flyers

Year

2025

Project summary

BreadHaus is an artisan bread brand born in New York City, blending modern design with the soul of the city. The identity plays off the contrast between old-world craft and urban energy. The logo is a custom bread tag, symbolizing everyday familiarity reimagined. It’s iconic, minimal, and instantly recognizable—evoking freshness, utility, and nostalgia all at once.

There are 4 locations: Soho, East Village, Bushwick, and Brooklyn. I've created a bread tag design for each, naming the crossroads on the top half. When you make a purchase, you will receive a bag to carry your bread which has been thoughtfully designed. Hanging from one of the handles is a location-specific tag—a tactile detail that connects each loaf to the neighborhood it came from, making every visit feel personal and place-based.

The main color palette pairs are chartreuse and chocolate brown. The secondary colors are lighter and muted tones of each. Chartreuse adds a jolt of vibrancy, reflecting NYC’s constant motion and edge. Chocolate brown grounds the brand, echoing the warmth of a crisp crust and neighborhood bakeries.

I was originally assigned to design a carefully crafted paper bag featuring a custom logo. From that starting point, I developed BreadHaus. Pushing beyond the brief, I expanded the identity system to include eight unique posters and flyers, designed for promotion on the streets of NYC.

A Day In My Life

Project

A receipt design reflecting a day in my life while highlighting societal issues like sexual violence, the climate crisis, and AI technology, with the goal of sparking conversations about the systems that harm innocent people.

Scope

Layout design, Social commentary, Typography

Year

2024

Project summary

I set out to create a piece that represented a day in my life that also used a font I designed. The top of the receipt notes the store number (my age), the store location (my part time address), and the time the cashier's drawer opens (when I wake up for the day). However, what follows this harmless text shows what my mind centers around as the day goes on.

Everything has a cost—whether it's my morning bagel ($4.60) or the number of women that are victims of sexual violence each day in the United States (1,270). My mind is constantly occupied by the rapid advancement of AI technology, the accelerating climate crisis, and the current leader of our country whose actions will leave scars for generations.

"I will not be complacent towards product prices according to card issuer agreement" concludes that I will not stand idly by the system that harms innocent people. As a designer I have the ability to make art that makes noise and starts conversations, and that was my goal with this piece.

Behind all the text, you’ll see my original font in red, displaying the phrase: “What world have I been brought into, and what am I going to do about it?”

Kinetik

Project

A font for the loud and the bold, making every word impossible to ignore.

Scope

Typeface development, Letterform design

Year

2024

Project summary

Kinetik is a san serif font inspired by thick display fonts that have the ability to be enlarged and fill a whole page. It's wide letterforms and limited spacing make it perfect for bold, impactful designs. Kinetik’s strong geometric structure is balanced with subtle curves, giving it a modern yet approachable feel.

font is designed for maximum legibility at large sizes, making it ideal for posters, headlines, and branding projects where attention-grabbing typography is key. Its limited spacing ensures a cohesive and compact look, while still maintaining clarity and character.

font was created using Glyph's in an Advanced Typography course.

AIGA Poster

Project

The classic FIND-a-WORD puzzle reimagined to create the 2024 AIGA Conference poster.

Scope

Poster design, Typography, Design vernacular

Year

2024

Project summary

In this project, I studied the concept of design vernacular. It refers to the unique visual language, style, or aesthetic specific to a particular culture, region, time period, or group. It is shaped by the traditions, beliefs, and environmental influences of the context it emerges from.

I used the Charlton's 1974 "FIND-a-WORD" puzzle page vernacular to create an informational poster on the 2024 AIGA Conference. This conference is a premier annual gathering for design professionals, hosted by the American Institute of Graphic Arts (AIGA). Circled in the word-search box includes the dates, year, and theme for the 2024 conference: Margins.

1974 Charlton's “FIND-a-WORD”

Charlton's Find-A-Word puzzle pages from 1974 reflect a straightforward design style typical of mass-market print media of the time—bold, all-caps typefaces, tightly arranged grids, and minimal ornamentation. The design prioritizes clarity and ease of use, with a slight handmade feel in the headings that gives it a personal touch. This is the design vernacular I went forward with.

Historically, word search puzzles gained popularity in the U.S. during the late 1960s and early '70s, becoming a staple in affordable puzzle books and magazines like Charlton's. These pages catered to casual readers, offering simple entertainment during a boom in leisure-based print culture.

3D Type

Project

Exploring the playful intersection of humor, language, and design by turning cheeky phrases into physical forms that highlight material, shape, and visual wit.

Scope

Typography, Sculptural Design

Year

2024

Project summary

The goal of this series was to recreate a word in 3-dimensions utilizing any material of my choice. I went with short and humorous phrases that corresponded to the materials being used. The playful nature of the visuals and the witty commentary acts as a great advertisement for each brand.

 

In an age where so much of typography lives on screens, I’m drawn to how type can exist physically—how it can be touched, held, or even consumed. 3D type invites new ways of interaction, storytelling, and visual impact, especially in spaces like branding, installations, packaging, or experiential design.
